I study how disease transmission networks are shaped by environmental processes, human–environment interactions, and the social and spatial contexts in which people live. My work spans epidemiology, medical geography, biostatistics, and disease ecology.

I graduated from Duke University with highest honors in Health and Medical Geography of Human–Environmental Interactions, and I'm currently pursuing a Fulbright–National Geographic award. When I'm not doing research, you might find me translating Swahili, performing gamelan, reading world literature, or exploring the world.
